## Clonal dynamics

Every tissue is a population of cells acquiring mutations throughout life. Some mutant clones drift; others are driven to expand by positive selection. We develop population-genetics models that describe how clones grow, compete and are purified, and we test them against deep-sequencing measurements from healthy and pre-cancerous tissue.

> How much of the variation we see between people reflects chance, and how much reflects selection acting on the mutations they happen to carry?

## Mutation & selection

Distinguishing driver mutations from passengers is central to understanding early cancer. We infer mutation rates and selection coefficients directly from data, using error-corrected sequencing to detect clones long before they become clinically apparent.

## Phylogenetic reconstruction

The mutations a cell carries record its history. From single-cell and bulk sequencing we reconstruct phylogenies that reveal when key events occurred, how lineages diverged, and how the architecture of a tissue changes with age and exposure.
